# Research notes: Market structure basics

These are draft working notes for Codex and future editorial review. They are not final reader-facing content and should not be treated as verified trading guidance.

## Research question

How should Lumen explain trend, range, higher highs/lows, lower highs/lows, break of structure, failed breakout, acceptance, and rejection without turning them into mechanical signals?

## Working summary

Market structure is a descriptive framework for organizing price movement. It can help frame indicator interpretation, but it is subjective and timeframe-dependent.

## Key claims

- Trend and range are contextual states, not permanent labels.
- Higher highs/higher lows and lower highs/lower lows are common descriptive concepts.
- Breaks of structure require confirmation and can fail.
- Acceptance and rejection should be observed through time, location, volume, and follow-through rather than assumed from a single print.

## Uncertainty / limits

- No statistical edge is established here.
- Definitions vary among traders and timeframes.
- Reader-facing pages should avoid “buy/sell when structure breaks” language.
