# MBSR and ACT self-practice boundaries

## Scope

This note supports public-safe boundaries for MBSR-informed and ACT-informed self-practice pages in Commonplace.

## MBSR-informed self-practice

Reader-facing pages may support:

- Understanding MBSR's structure.
- Short body scan, sitting practice, and informal mindfulness.
- Reflection on stress reactions.
- Safe shortening, stopping, and changing practice objects.
- Practice logs and motivation.

Reader-facing pages must not claim:

- To deliver an official MBSR course.
- To replace trained instructors, group inquiry, intake, or clinical judgment.
- To treat medical or mental health conditions.
- That more practice is always better.

## ACT-informed self-practice

Reader-facing pages may support:

- Defusion-style phrasing.
- Values clarification.
- Small committed actions.
- Present-moment grounding.
- Reflection on emotion and action.

Reader-facing pages must not claim:

- To deliver ACT therapy.
- To diagnose or treat conditions.
- To replace therapist-guided ACT.
- That acceptance means enduring unsafe situations.
- That emotional distress should be handled alone.

## Stop / consult conditions

Summarize boundaries already present in meditation safety notes:

- self-harm or harm risk
- suicidality
- severe depression
- mania or hypomania-like activation
- psychosis-like symptoms
- severe dissociation
- derealization / depersonalization
- trauma activation
- panic escalation
- sleep worsening
- functional impairment

## Reader-facing safe wording

- 「このページは、公式MBSRコースやACT療法の代替ではありません。」
- 「自己実践で扱うのは、短い気づき、記録、価値の確認、小さい行動選択までです。」
- 「強い症状、安全リスク、医療・心理療法に関わる判断は、自己流で扱わず専門的支援を優先します。」
- 「受容は危険な状況に居続けることではありません。」
- 「休む、やめる、距離を取る、助けを求めることも、価値に沿った行動になりえます。」
