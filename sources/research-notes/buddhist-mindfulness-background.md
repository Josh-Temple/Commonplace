# Buddhist mindfulness background

## Scope

This note records Buddhist-background terms that are often mentioned in modern mindfulness writing. It is not a doctrinal authority and must not be used to claim clinical efficacy.

## Key terms and texts

- **Satipaṭṭhāna**: often translated as foundations or establishments of mindfulness; associated with contemplation of body, feeling tone, mind, and dhammas. Key candidate: MN 10 / DN 22 on SuttaCentral.
- **Ānāpānasati**: mindfulness of breathing. Key candidate: MN 118 on SuttaCentral.
- **Mettā**: loving-kindness or goodwill. Key candidate: Karaṇīya Mettā Sutta / Snp 1.8 on SuttaCentral.
- **Vipassanā**: insight-oriented practice traditions; modern usage varies and should not be collapsed into one method.
- **Dhammapada**: verse anthology; useful for historical/religious background, not clinical evidence.

## Clinical translation cautions

- Buddhist practices sit in ethical, communal, philosophical, and soteriological contexts.
- MBSR, MBCT, and ACT borrow or parallel some attentional and acceptance skills but are secular clinical/psychological programs.
- A sutta can document a traditional practice lineage; it does not prove modern health claims.
