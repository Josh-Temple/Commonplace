# Mindfulness practice motivation and habit

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about sustaining mindfulness practice without perfectionism or unsafe intensification.

## Motivation themes

- practice as training awareness and choice
- practice as reducing automaticity
- practice as returning to values
- practice as building a pause before reaction
- practice as learning safe self-observation

Reader-facing motivation should say people practice to create space between reaction and behavior, not to become emotionless or perfectly calm. Buddhist or ethical motivations such as goodwill, non-reactivity, wisdom, and understanding suffering can be named as background but should not be treated as clinical outcome evidence.

## Habit and adherence

- small sessions
- consistent cues
- implementation intentions
- practice logs
- weekly review
- adjustment rather than self-blame
- stopping or reducing practice when adverse signs appear

## Evidence notes

- Parsons et al. 2017 supports treating home practice in MBSR/MBCT as relevant, but the evidence should be worded cautiously because practice type, quantity, adherence, measurement, and causality vary.
- Habit formation / implementation intention literature is plausible support for cues and small plans, but this note has not yet verified specific studies enough to cite precise effect sizes or timelines.
- ACT values and committed action sources support linking mindfulness to values-based behavior rather than only symptom monitoring.
- Self-compassion or self-criticism sources need direct review before claiming that practice reliably reduces self-criticism.

## Cautions

- Do not imply that failure to continue means weak will.
- Do not recommend increasing duration when practice worsens symptoms.
- Do not make practice streaks the primary goal.
- Do not turn practice into self-monitoring obsession.
- Do not encourage private diaries or sensitive personal records in a public repository.

## Reader-facing safe wording

- “中断は失敗ではありません。再開する時は、前回の時間に戻そうとせず、2分から再開します。”
- “継続できたかだけでなく、安全に調整できたかを見ます。”
- “長くするのは、睡眠、不安、現実感、衝動性が安定している時だけにします。”
- “悪化している時は、意志で押し切らず、短くする・対象を変える・休む・相談するを選びます。”

## Needs verification

- Specific implementation-intention and habit-formation sources.
- Self-efficacy sources for mindfulness practice adherence.
- Detailed home-practice dose-response evidence in MBSR/MBCT.
