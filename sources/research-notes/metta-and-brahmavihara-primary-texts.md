# Mettā and brahmavihāra primary texts

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about mettā and the brahmavihāras in Theravada-related contexts.

It is not a complete doctrinal review, practice authorization, or clinical-efficacy review.

## Key terms

- **mettā**: goodwill, friendliness, loving-kindness, or benevolence depending on translation.
- **karuṇā**: compassion, often associated with responsiveness to suffering.
- **muditā**: appreciative joy or sympathetic joy.
- **upekkhā**: equanimity; not indifference.
- **brahmavihāra**: “divine abidings” or sublime states; fourfold set of mettā, karuṇā, muditā, upekkhā.

## Primary texts

- Snp 1.8 Karaṇīya Mettā Sutta / Karaṇīyamettasutta.
- Other brahmavihāra suttas were not reviewed in detail in this pass.

## Reader-facing safe framing

- Mettā is not forced warm emotion.
- Mettā is not forgiveness on demand.
- Mettā is not reconciliation.
- Mettā is not boundary removal.
- Difficult-person practice should be handled cautiously.
- Buddhist mettā and modern loving-kindness interventions are related but not identical.
- The sutta supports ethical and contemplative background, not modern clinical-effect claims.
