# Theravada lineages and modern vipassanā

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about modern Theravada-related meditation lineages and adaptations. It is not a lineage manual, retreat guide, or claim of authority.

It records distinctions to preserve so Commonplace does not collapse Pāli-canon textual framing, commentarial framing, Burmese vipassanā, Goenka retreats, Thai Forest teaching, and contemporary secular mindfulness into one method.

## Distinctions to preserve

- Pāli-canon / Nikāya textual framing.
- Commentarial / Visuddhimagga framing.
- Modern Burmese vipassanā.
- Mahasi Sayadaw lineage.
- Ledi Sayadaw influence.
- U Ba Khin / S. N. Goenka lay retreat system.
- Thai Forest approaches.
- Contemporary secular mindfulness adaptations.

## Questions to answer

### What does each tradition emphasize?

Current verified detail is incomplete. Safe reader-facing pages may say that different Theravada-related lineages emphasize different combinations of mindfulness, concentration, noting, body awareness, breath, ethical discipline, monastic training, insight, and lay-retreat formats.

### How do they frame mindfulness, concentration, insight, noting, body awareness, or breath?

Mahasi-related materials are commonly associated with noting and systematic vipassanā practice, but precise instructions should remain outside Commonplace. U Ba Khin / Goenka materials are associated with lay retreat systems and body scanning, but Commonplace should not reproduce retreat instructions. Thai Forest sources emphasize monastic discipline, direct practice, and teacher-specific language; they should not be reduced to one method.

### What should not be generalized from one lineage to all Theravada meditation?

- Noting practice.
- Body scanning.
- Breath concentration.
- Open monitoring.
- Lay ten-day retreat format.
- Strict jhāna criteria.
- Thai Forest idioms about practice.

### What is safe to mention in reader-facing pages?

- There are multiple modern Theravada-related lineages.
- “Vipassanā” is not one single standardized method.
- Modern retreat methods are not identical to early Buddhist texts.
- Thai Forest, Burmese vipassanā, and secular mindfulness translations differ in vocabulary and aim.

### What requires teacher guidance or deeper source review?

- Retreat instructions.
- Noting technique details.
- Body scanning technique details.
- Jhāna standards and attainments.
- Stream-entry or insight-knowledge maps.
- Strong no-self, death, or body-parts contemplations.

## Reader-facing safe wording

- 「現代ヴィパッサナー運動には複数の系譜があり、一つの方法としてまとめません。」
- 「Mahasi系、U Ba Khin / Goenka系、Thai Forest系、経典ベースの説明は、重なる語彙を使っていても同じ実践体系とは限りません。」
- 「Commonplaceでは、系譜差を混同しないための地図として扱い、流派の優劣や実践指導は扱いません。」
- 「現代マインドフルネスへの翻訳は、上座部仏教系の倫理・智慧・非執着の文脈をすべて保持するとは限りません。」

## Claims to avoid

- “Vipassanā is one single method.”
- “Mahasi / Goenka / Thai Forest represents all Theravada meditation.”
- “Modern retreat methods are identical to early Buddhist texts.”
- “Noting, body scanning, breath concentration, and open monitoring are interchangeable.”
- “One lineage is the correct or superior Theravada meditation.”
