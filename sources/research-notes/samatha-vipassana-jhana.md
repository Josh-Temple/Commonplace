# Samatha, vipassanā, and jhāna

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about serenity, insight, and absorption/concentration in Theravada-related meditation contexts.

It is not an attainment map, practice manual, teacher authorization, or clinical evidence review.

## Key terms

- **samatha**: serenity, tranquility, calming, or settling cultivation; not mere relaxation.
- **vipassanā**: insight or clear seeing; not generic open monitoring.
- **jhāna**: absorption or deep meditative concentration in Buddhist contexts; not a casual focus technique.
- **samādhi**: concentration, collectedness, or unification; translation and doctrinal usage vary.
- **sati**: mindfulness, recollection, keeping in mind; broader Buddhist context than secular attention training.
- **paññā**: wisdom or discernment; connected with insight and liberation-oriented understanding.

## Main cautions

- Do not present samatha and vipassanā as always completely separate techniques.
- Do not reduce vipassanā to generic open monitoring.
- Do not reduce samatha to mere relaxation.
- Do not give jhāna instructions as casual self-help.
- Do not claim attainment or diagnose meditative states.
- Do not use modern retreat branding to define all Theravada practice.

## Traditional and modern differences

- **Early Buddhist / Nikāya framing**: samatha and vipassanā can be read as qualities or aspects developed in practice, not always separate branded techniques.
- **Commentarial / Visuddhimagga framing**: needs further review before detailed Commonplace claims.
- **Modern Burmese vipassanā traditions**: Mahasi, Ledi, U Ba Khin / Goenka, and related lay retreat forms should not be merged into one identical system.
- **Thai Forest approaches**: Ajahn Chah, Ajahn Sumedho, Ajahn Sucitto, Ajahn Brahm, Thanissaro Bhikkhu, and related teachers differ in language and emphasis.
- **Lay retreat / contemporary mindfulness adaptations**: may translate terms into attention, insight, or stress language; do not treat this as the whole tradition.

## Reader-facing safe wording

- 「samathaは単なるリラックス法ではなく、心を静め、安定させる訓練として語られます。」
- 「vipassanāは現代心理学のopen monitoringと重なる説明をされることがありますが、仏教的には洞察・智慧・非執着の文脈を持ちます。」
- 「jhānaはカジュアルな集中テクニックとして教えず、到達判定もしません。」
- 「samathaとvipassanāは、伝統や解釈によって関係づけが異なります。完全に別技法として固定しません。」
