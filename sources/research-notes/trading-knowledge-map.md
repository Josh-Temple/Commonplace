# Research notes: Trading knowledge map

These are draft working notes for Codex and future editorial review. They are not final reader-facing content and should not be treated as verified trading guidance.

## Research question

How should Lumen organize public-safe trading education around context, structure, levels, volume, entry process, invalidation, risk, emotional control, and review?

## Working summary

The trading area should be a decision-structure map, not a trading system. It can organize how a trader studies market context, important levels, volume-profile references, risk, and post-trade review while avoiding personalized advice and guaranteed outcomes.

## Key claims

- Trading content should separate definitions, interpretation, heuristics, personal rules, and uncertainty.
- A complete decision process includes context, level, scenario, trigger, invalidation, risk/reward, emotional check, decision, and review.
- Emotional control and rule adherence are central because poor execution can invalidate even a well-defined setup.
- Post-trade review should distinguish process quality from outcome.

## Uncertainty / limits

- This note does not validate any profitable strategy.
- It does not provide personalized financial advice.
- Risk management, journaling, behavioral finance, and trading psychology sources still need verification.

## Candidate sources to verify

- Risk management and trade journaling books or courses from reputable market educators.
- Behavioral finance / trading psychology materials.
- Exchange or broker educational material that clearly separates education from advice.
- User-provided source notes, if added later.
