# Research notes: Horizontal levels basics

These are draft working notes for Codex and future editorial review. They are not final reader-facing content and should not be treated as verified trading guidance.

## Research question

How should Lumen explain horizontal levels as decision areas rather than magic lines or standalone support/resistance signals?

## Working summary

Horizontal levels are commonly drawn from prior highs/lows, swing points, daily/weekly levels, round numbers, session highs/lows, and volume-profile references. They should be framed as areas where decisions may occur, not guaranteed reversal or breakout points.

## Key claims

- Prior highs/lows and swing highs/lows are common reference points.
- Daily/weekly levels and session extremes can matter because many participants may observe them.
- Round numbers may attract attention but are not inherently predictive.
- Confluence with POC, VAH, VAL, or market structure can make a level worth observing.

## Uncertainty / limits

- This note does not prove any level has predictive power.
- Level selection can be subjective and prone to hindsight bias.
- More lines can create false confidence.

## Candidate sources to verify

- Technical analysis textbooks or educational materials on support/resistance.
- Exchange/platform education about session highs/lows.
- Volume Profile documentation for POC/VAH/VAL confluence.

## Notes for Codex

Phrase levels as decision areas and observation prompts. Do not turn them into trade calls.
