# Body scan and interoception

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about body scan practice, bodily awareness, and interoception in mindfulness-based programs.

## Working definitions

- Body scan practice involves moving attention through regions of the body.
- Interoception broadly refers to sensing internal bodily states.
- Body scan in MBSR should not be reduced to relaxation or symptom removal.

## Evidence notes

- Brown University describes MBSR as an 8-week program that teaches mindfulness meditation and movement practices, with orientation, instructor-led sessions, home practice, and one all-day session. The public page does not by itself establish body scan outcomes.
- Existing MBSR descriptions commonly include body scan, sitting meditation, and mindful movement as formal practices, but platform/program descriptions should be separated from efficacy evidence.
- Interoception and body awareness are plausible mechanisms or training targets in some mindfulness literature, but this note does not yet verify enough peer-reviewed mechanism studies to support strong claims.
- Reader-facing claims should say body scan may train attention to bodily sensations and reactions, not that it reliably improves interoception or treats symptoms.

## Safety notes

- Internal bodily attention can be destabilizing for some people.
- Pain, panic, trauma activation, dissociation, health anxiety, or sleep disruption may require modification.
- External grounding, eyes open, movement, shorter practice, or stopping practice may be safer for some readers.
- Pain should not be framed as something to observe indefinitely or override.
- Breath-focused practice may be difficult for some readers, but body-focused practice can also be difficult; external sounds, visual contact, walking, or ordinary action may be safer alternatives.

## Needs verification

- Peer-reviewed sources specifically connecting body scan practice to interoception, body awareness, and clinical outcomes.
- MBSR curriculum/manual sources that specify body scan sequencing and home-practice expectations.
- Trauma-sensitive body scan adaptations from clinical or training sources.
- Evidence on when interoceptive attention worsens panic, health anxiety, dissociation, or trauma symptoms.

## Reader-facing safe wording

- “ボディスキャンは身体感覚への気づきを育てる練習として扱える。”
- “リラックスできないことや何も感じないことも経験として扱う。”
- “身体感覚への注意が不安や記憶を強める場合は、短くする、外部対象へ移る、中止する、相談する。”
