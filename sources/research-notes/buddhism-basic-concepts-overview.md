# Buddhism basic concepts overview

## Scope

This note supports broad reader-facing pages about basic Buddhist concepts in Commonplace. It is not a doctrinal authority, sectarian overview, philological study, or clinical-efficacy review.

## Pages supported

- `content/indexes/buddhism.md`
- `content/concepts/buddhism-four-noble-truths.md`
- `content/concepts/buddhism-noble-eightfold-path.md`
- `content/concepts/buddhism-five-precepts.md`
- `content/concepts/buddhism-five-hindrances.md`

## Core boundary

The Buddhism pages are broad conceptual maps. The Theravada meditation pages are more specific meditation-context maps. They should link to each other but not collapse into one cluster.

The broad pages may give short orientations to four noble truths, noble eightfold path, five precepts, and five hindrances. More specific meditation-context claims should defer to the Theravada source notes and reader-facing pages.

## Concepts covered

- Four noble truths / four truths.
- Noble eightfold path.
- Five precepts.
- Five hindrances.
- Relationship to Theravada meditation pages.
- Relationship to modern mindfulness and psychological self-regulation.

## Claims to avoid

- Treating Buddhism as a self-help productivity system.
- Treating four truths as a simple emotional regulation tool.
- Treating eightfold path as a generic life-hack checklist.
- Treating five precepts as private moral judgment of the reader.
- Treating Buddhist concepts as clinical evidence.
- Presenting broad Buddhism pages as complete doctrinal explanations.
- Collapsing broad Buddhism pages and Theravada meditation-context pages into one role.
