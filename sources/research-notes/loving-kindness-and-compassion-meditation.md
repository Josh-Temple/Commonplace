# Loving-kindness and compassion meditation

## Scope

This note supports cautious reader-facing content about loving-kindness meditation, compassion meditation, and metta-style practices. It does not establish clinical efficacy for any individual reader.

## Working definitions

- Loving-kindness meditation commonly uses repeated phrases of goodwill toward oneself, benefactors/loved ones, neutral people, difficult people, and wider groups of beings.
- Compassion meditation often emphasizes sensitivity to suffering and a wish for suffering to be relieved.
- Buddhist metta background should be separated from contemporary psychological intervention evidence.

## Evidence notes checked

- Galante et al. (2014) systematically reviewed and meta-analyzed kindness-based meditation RCTs. The abstract reports benefits versus passive controls on some self-reported outcomes, but intervention forms and comparators vary.
- Zeng et al. (2015) reviewed loving-kindness meditation and positive emotions. This supports cautious language that LKM may influence positive emotions, not a guaranteed result.
- Later reviews/meta-analyses exist for life satisfaction, anxiety, compassion, and neuroimaging outcomes, but these need closer reading before stronger claims are promoted into `content/`.

## Practice cautions

- Self-directed loving-kindness can be difficult for people with intense self-criticism or trauma histories.
- Moving quickly to a “difficult person” can intensify anger, fear, shame, or trauma reactions.
- Loving-kindness should not be framed as forgiveness, reconciliation, or loss of boundaries.

## Priority sources checked

- Galante J, Galante I, Bekkers MJ, Gallacher J. “Effect of kindness-based meditation on health and well-being: a systematic review and meta-analysis.” *Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology*. 2014. PubMed: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24979314/
- Zeng X, Chiu CP, Wang R, Oei TP, Leung FY. “The effect of loving-kindness meditation on positive emotions: a meta-analytic review.” *Frontiers in Psychology*. 2015. PubMed Central: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4630307/
- SuttaCentral Snp 1.8 Karaṇīyamettasutta. https://suttacentral.net/snp1.8

## Needs verification

- Recent meta-analyses on loving-kindness / compassion meditation for anxiety, life satisfaction, and self-compassion should be reviewed directly before adding stronger effect claims.
- More sources are needed on contraindications or adaptations for trauma and high self-criticism.
